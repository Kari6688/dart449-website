As we may think by Vannevar Bush


In light of the end of WW II, this text touches upon the technologies created for the atomic bombs towards the end of the war. What I found interesting is the parallel between the way people work on technologies now and then. This article brings out new ideas and methods form collaborative work. The scientists witnessed that diversity can bring more to the table and working all in one space makes things a lot easier. With the memex machine, Bush undoubtedly influenced the future of computers and the internet itself. In contrast to the software and apps we use now, almost everything is designed to be a collaborative experience. In the web and design field, collaboration is now key. Figma for example is a software that thrives on sharing knowledge and resources with the community and working on the projects seamlessly with other people.

The Social Dilema


"The Social Dilemma" is a documentary that explores the dark side of social media and its impact on society. It features interviews with former executives and engineers from companies like Facebook, Google, and Twitter who reveal how these platforms use algorithms to manipulate user behavior and monetize their attention. The documentary highlights how social media can be addictive, polarizing, and contribute to the spread of misinformation. It also raises concerns about the impact of social media on mental health, privacy, and democracy. It addresses the negative effects of social media and raises questions about the danger of data collection, what are the actual benefits of social media, and what impact it has on mental health and well-being of it's users.

“Thank You, Black Twitter”: State Violence, Digital Counterpublics, and Pedagogies of Resistance


This article explores how Black Twitter serves as a digital counterpublic and a site of resistance against state violence. It analyzes the power of digital counterpublics to challenge dominant narratives and mobilize communities around issues of social justice. The article also highlights the pedagogical potential of digital counterpublics and raises important ethical questions about the use of digital technologies. Overall, it highlights the potential of digital technologies to create spaces of resistance and promote social justice.

Richard Stallman, The GNU Manifesto


Richard Stallman's "GNU Manifesto" advocates for the creation of free and open-source software that is accessible and modifiable by users. It emphasizes the importance of preserving users' freedom and privacy, the distinction between "free software" and "open-source software," and the need for collective action and collaboration to achieve these goals. The manifesto reflects Stallman's belief in promoting ethical principles in software development and the use of technology for the common good.Stallman founded the GNU project to develop a complete operating system composed entirely of free software. He also advocated for the preservation of users' freedom and privacy, particularly in the face of increasing centralization and control by corporate entities.
