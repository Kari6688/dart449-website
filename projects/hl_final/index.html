1

The High Line
was designed in collaboration with James Corner Field Operations and Piet Oudolf. Together, they created a 1.5 mile park build on an abandoned railroad stretching from lower Manhattan to the Meatpacking District.

2

The design
of the High Line varies from paving and vegetation. The paving system consists of individually pre-casted concrete planks with open joints to promote emergent growth. The design raises various civic issues like the reclamation of public spaces, adaptive reuse and preservation.

3

The Spur
is the newest addition to the High Line. The overall project inspired other cities worldwide to rethink their global development models. After the feedback gather regarding the previous phases of the High Line, the Spur was made by the people for the people.

4

Mid-1800s

Trains ran on street level tracks to deliver food to lower Manhattan. 10th Avenue became “Death Avenue” with more 540 people killed by trains.

 1911 - 1924

West Side Improvement project started by removing street-level crossings. Later the plan ordered to remove the tracks from the street and create an elevated rail line.

 1933

The first train ran on the High Line which was then called the “West Side Elevate Line”. The line was fully functional in 1934 transporting meat, dairy and produce.

 1960 - 1980

The rise in the truck industry created a decline in rail commerce. The abandon and call for the High Line demolition followed.

 1999

CSX Transportation, owner of the High Line opened to the proposal of structure reuse.

 2003 - 2009

To start a dialogue about the High Line an idea competition was held which helped bring awareness. In 2009, CSX Transportation donated ownership of the structure to the City of New York and the first section of the High Line was open to the public.

 Today

With the second section opened in 2012 and the Spur in 2019, the High Line redefines the idea of green and public spaces. It utilized it’s 1.45 mile long greenway to connect neighbours, food, art and plants featuring 500+ species. The park is maintained and operated by Friends of the High Line.
